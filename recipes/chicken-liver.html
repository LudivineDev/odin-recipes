Chicken Liver Mousse

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken livers
  • 1 Tbsp oil (for high-heat cooking, like canola)
  • 2 Tbsp butter (divided)
  • 350g sliced shallots
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)
  • 1/4 cup cognac (or brandy, port, madeira, wine, or stock)
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tsp balsamic vinegar

Instructions

  1. Rinse, dry, and trim the chicken livers. Set a large pan over medium-high heat. Add oil and 1 Tbsp butter.
  2. Season livers with salt and pepper. Once the pan is hot, add livers and cook until golden on both sides (about 2 minutes per side). The internal temperature should reach 150Â°F (very pink inside). Remove from pan.
  3. Reduce heat to low. Add remaining butter and let it melt. Add shallots and salt, stirring occasionally.
  4. Once brown bits form at the bottom of the pan, deglaze with cognac and scrape up browned bits. Continue cooking until shallots are soft and jammy (about 15-20 minutes), adding oil if the pan becomes dry.
  5. Stir in balsamic vinegar. Taste and adjust seasoning. Remove 1/3 of the shallots for garnish.
  6. Add 1/4 cup heavy cream to the pan, bring to a simmer, and scrape up browned bits.
  7. For well-done livers, return them to the pan and cook, stirring frequently, until no longer pink inside. Let cool until warm.
  8. Blend livers, shallots, and remaining 1/2 cup heavy cream in a blender or food processor until smooth.
  9. Spoon into containers and cover with plastic wrap, pressing it onto the surface, or seal with clarified butter.
  10. Refrigerate overnight. Serve with reserved shallots, crostini, fruit jam, or pickled shallots.

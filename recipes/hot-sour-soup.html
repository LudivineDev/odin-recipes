Hot and Sour Soup

Ingredients

  • 8 cups chicken broth or vegetable broth
  • 8 ounces shiitake mushrooms (or baby bella mushrooms), thinly-sliced with stems discarded
  • 1 (8-ounce) can bamboo shoots, drained (optional)
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar, or more to taste Chinkiang vinegar or Zhenjiang vinegar
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon chili garlic sauce
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 2 large eggs, whisked
  • 8 ounces firm tofu, cut into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 4 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil
  • Kosher salt and white pepper (or black pepper)

Instructions

  1. Set aside ¼ cup of the chicken or vegetable broth for later use.
  2. In a large stock pot, add the remaining 7 ¾ cups of chicken or vegetable broth, mushrooms, bamboo shoots (if using), rice vinegar, soy sauce, ginger, and chili garlic sauce. Stir to combine and heat over medium-high heat until the soup reaches a simmer.
  3. While the soup is heating, whisk together the reserved ¼ cup of broth and cornstarch in a small bowl until completely smooth.
  4. Once the soup has reached a simmer, stir in the cornstarch mixture and stir for about 1 minute until the soup has thickened.
  5. Continue stirring in a circular motion, then drizzle in the whisked eggs in a thin stream (while still stirring the soup) to create egg ribbons.
  6. Stir in the tofu, half of the green onions, and sesame oil.
  7. Season the soup with salt and a pinch of white or black pepper to taste. If you’d like a more “sour” soup, add additional rice vinegar. For a spicier soup, add more chili garlic sauce.
  8. Serve immediately, garnished with the remaining green onions.

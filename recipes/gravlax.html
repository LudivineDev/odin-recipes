Salmon Gravlax

Ingredients

  • Fresh Atlantic or Pacific salmon, or trout
  • 1 bunch of fresh dill
  • Freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
  • Fine sea salt
  • Maple syrup
  • 1 lime (zest only)
  • Kitchen scale (to weigh the fish)
  • 1 large ziploc bag

Instructions

  1. Freeze the salmon for at least 24 hours to kill any parasites. Thaw it in the fridge before starting the curing process.

  2. Peel the lime. Set aside the zest.
  3. Weigh the salmon using a kitchen scale. Multiply the weight by 0.025 (2.5%) to determine the amount of salt needed.
  4. Rub both sides of the salmon evenly with the measured salt, lime zest, black pepper, chopped dill, and a drizzle of maple syrup.
  5. Place the seasoned salmon into a ziploc bag, seal it, and refrigerate for 12 to 24 hours.
  6. Remove the salmon from the bag. Discard the dill and lime zest, then pat the fish dry on both sides using paper towels.
  7. Place the salmon on a wire rack (over a tray or plate) in the fridge and let it air-dry for at least 4 hours.
  8. After drying, the salmon is ready to slice and serve. Enjoy!
